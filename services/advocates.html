2024 · Environment & Land Court

Procedural Competence Before the Environment & Land Court

Year2024
CourtEnvironment & Land Court (ELC)
JurisdictionKenya
Focus AreaProcedural Competence & Land Law

In 2024, TLB Tuyia Advocates was engaged in proceedings before Kenya's Environment and Land Court — a specialised court established under Article 162(2)(b) of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010 to hear and determine disputes relating to the environment and land.

The matter centred on questions of procedural competence — specifically, whether the correct procedural frameworks had been followed in initiating and prosecuting the suit, and whether the court had been properly seized of jurisdiction to determine the matter on its merits. Such questions require a thorough command of both the Environment and Land Court Act, the Civil Procedure Rules, and established Kenyan case law on jurisdiction and locus standi.

TLB Tuyia Advocates provided rigorous legal research, filed substantive pleadings, and appeared to argue the procedural points before the Court — demonstrating the firm's capacity to handle technically demanding Environment and Land Court litigation with professional precision.

Firm Capability Demonstrated: Procedural advocacy before the Environment & Land Court, jurisdictional argumentation, and substantive land law litigation — underpinning TLB Tuyia Advocates' credibility in complex environment and property disputes.
